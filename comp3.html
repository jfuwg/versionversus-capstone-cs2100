Mario 64: Original VS Remake

Ahh...now this is a game I love far too much. I was too young to grow up with the original, but I grew up with the remake. Eventually I came around to loving original too. But enough about me, the original came out on (who could've guessed?) the Nintendo 64 in 1996 while the DS remake came out 2004. Okay...I for one, did not realize there was less than a decade between those 2. It is profoundly shocking to me how much gaming and society changed in that short space of time, and also because remakes usually take more time to come out. One looks ancient to me while the other is what I grew up with. What I assume is that they realized they could port over a classic but the DS having 2 screens means it was better to spice it up, and they poured so much spice in that it became a radically different game.

The DS having 2 screens doesn't always take advantage of it, as most of the time it just shows the map in this game. Visually the screenshot nor video does justice to how it looks on a tiny DS screen (trust me it looks charming.) I wouldn't say the original is ugly but it's definitely a product of its time. I also wouldn't say the DS looks better though, sometimes the colors (like in the pic above) don't do it for me.

The main gameplay remains largely the same BUT, there is so much additional content like levels and minigames, and new characters! Remember when unlocking Luigi was the biggest myth of its time? Well you now can in 64 DS. Remember the myths about unlocking Wario and Yoshi too? No? Oh..Well they're in DS as well. In fact, you have to play as Yoshi first and unlock Mario after! That is one gripe I have, as I don't find Yoshi nearly as fun, and there are certain sections added or changed that require you to switch to a character which just complicates things rather than makes it fun in my opinion. This does make for a more distinct idendity though. The little minigames are actually my favorite things as a child, they even take advantage of the 2 screens.

I adore so much that the DS version adds, like how you have to do a mini level inside of a level to unlock Mario, Luigi, and Wario. I loved the extra stars to unlock and everything but somehow, I ended up liking the original more. It was a long time before I could explain to myself why this could be but I realized it. The new content just gets in the way. None of them are long but the straightforwardness of the original just wins me out. The community for the original is still so big and that plays a part too.

The controls seem to be an issue for people on both versions...but the DS controls are definitively worse. I love how Mario moves around in the original but it's different and strange on the DS. It is also because of different physics in the DS and it just doesn't satisfy me as much.

All in all, I love both games for different reasons and I would totally say that it is worth checking out both because you will have a very different but welcome experience I would say. The N64 version has been ported several times, while the DS version doesn't get as much love which might be telling. But..although I might be blinded by nostalgia, the DS version is just something any Mario 64 needs to play. Go on, go play the games and have fun!
