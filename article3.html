Don't Forget About CRT's

Remember those blocky tvs/monitors that would weight a ton? You do? Great. You don't? Uh oh, you probably think they're obsolete and ancient huh? Well..they certaintly aren't being produced any longer so you're not completely wrong, as LCDs are the standard. But before LCDs were even a thing, all people could use was CRTs. Not to get into specifics but basically it uses a different technology to display an image on screen, and so the displays can look pretty different.

But what's the big deal you say? You've seen the scanlines on these things, thank god we have clear LCD screens now!...except these scanlines actually were important for at least one thing, videogames. We celebrate the retro "pixel" art style today and we see screenshots and think "huh, it looks blocky" but that wasn't exactly so back then. LCDs are so 'clear' to the point we see all those individual pixels, but CRTs actually smoothed out those pixels (somewhat, to varying degrees). The result, especially from afar, is a smoother looking art style. Even in early 3D games, CRTs help vastly in hiding imperfections and making the game look better.

Just based on this, we can see how the raw pixels look against the same image on a CRT screen. Notice how the right looks "blended" in a sense, and creates depth and a clear facial structure. This is what people were actually seeing back then. Many play on emulators nowadays, on LCD screens, and they experience those raw pixels. In a sense, they don't get the original visual experience. Developers worked with the CRT tech to complete their vision, so all those pixel art images online aren't how they would really appear back then. I remember getting my first CRT, and I couldn't believe how a simple pixelized water fountain suddenly looked like actual water flowing on my screen, glistening and all. Even without an actual CRT, there are filters to get the intended look in emulators (though its not as good). 3D PS1 and N64 games always looked so undercooked and raw lookswise when I played them at first, but on a CRT their imperfections are softened, and I see how appealing they really look.

I see people confused on why things looked "better" back then even though we have HD now, and the answer usually lies in how CRT's despite their flaws, had a great display. These things aren't being produced, but they're still out there. I highly reccomend that whomever reads this gets one for their setup. Search on Facebook Marketplace, thrift stores, flea markets, and E-Recycling centers if you can. Ebay is usually a no go, too pricey when theres always free/cheap ones laying around. My point in closing, DON'T dismiss this technology, it can be a game changer and give you the authentic presentation of a game's visuals. At the very least, remember that CRTs were actually pretty dang cool.
