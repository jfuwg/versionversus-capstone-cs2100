Silent Hill 2: Original VS Remake

Ah now this is a good game to compare. The original game came out on PS2 back in 2002. The remake came out in 2024, so there is 22 years between these releases. Development philosphies change in that time, and the studio behind the Demon Souls Remake was responible for the remake. Being the most famous in the franchise, the demand was there, and they went for it. The result is the ultimate representation of the state of remakes today.

The OG has the classic fixed camera angles with tank controls, a staple of the horror game genre in its day while the remake has an over the shoulder dynamic camera.

This creates a very different feeling while playing the game as the remake doesn't give me as much anxiety or claustrophobia. Graphically the remake is stunning, but the grimy noisy look of the original is not replicated. The fog especially is something PS2s did very well! The remake can look pretty gruesome at times with all the gory details.

Stylistically, these screenshots encapsulate their differences. I'd describe James, the main character, as appearing to see the main character, as appearing to harbor a deep, dark side to him. It's as though he's looking at a man changed by some pivotal event. But the remake makes him look anxious, and somewhat distressed. The remake always takes these creative liberties but never justifies it or makes it look as good. I just never felt as scared during the remake.

Gameplay wise, I was amazed by how fun the remake actually was in comparison to the OG. You see, the OG is very clunky at times, something it shared with many other games. But also, there is just more of a focus on combat in the remake. In the remake you feel like a bad MF taking out these monsters...but in the original I always felt like it was a test of survival, everytime I encountered a monster. Yes the combat is more fun in the remake but I think they went a little overboard with the focus on it, especially considering how they lengthened the game. This is one of the biggest differences as the original can be beaten in just 4-7 hours, but the remake is at about 12-15 hours. Unfortunately I think this just works against the remake, as the added content just feels like padding that it doesn't need. The originals tight compact experience is altered for a more appealing gameplay style.

It especially sucks that the remake removes the DLC "Born From A Wish" which is an extra 1 or 2 hours of content, with an important plot. I think it's important to know that you can't get the full experience with the remake. All in all the original is just better in my opinion, and I would reccomend to try the remake after. I still enjoyed it after all. Both have their own worth. Ideally you'd have a CRT and a PS2 for the original but...an emulator on whatever you use can work too. Try it out, Silent Hill 2 is a classic for a reason.
