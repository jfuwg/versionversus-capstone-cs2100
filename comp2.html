Final Fantasy 7: Original VS Remake

First off, this article will only cover the first Final Fantasy 7 Remake, as it is split in 3 games, and only 2 are out. This first part extends the first 4-5 hours of the OG game into a full 40 hour game. Somehow the 2nd part is even longer...but they each deserve their own article. Setting some context, the OG game came out in 1997 on PS1 while the first remake came out in 2020 for PS4. Being one of the most influential games ever, it was only inetivitable that people wanted this game in new packaging, and so they began their work and announced it in 2015. 5 years to release was a long time, but they wanted to put their upmost effort into this.

To many people's surprise, this remake reinvisions the story and gameplay radically. Many wanted a 1:1 remake with better graphics but this new experience is more interesting in my opinion. 1:1 remakes are boring to me.

Story wise, it follows the original quite closely, but in a more expanded sense of course. It really lets you take in the city state of Midgar more than OG FF7 could. After all, it is the only setting of the game in part 1, while part 2 focuses on the open world. Sooner than later, the story suddenly introduces an element that, in non spoiler terms, makes it later clear that this game is intended to be played after the original. It's fine to play this first but from what I have played from Remake and Rebirth(part 2), you will appreciate this game infinitely more if you have played the OG game alongside the prequel Crisis Core. The OG is a classic for a reason, don't think the remake replaces it for one bit. After all, the remake trilogy isn't even finished.

But you came here to check out the differences right? Well most obviously the remake looks gorgeous. I don't always like the colors and lighting but it really does make you feel like you're in the slums, and every character looks just like how a kid back then would imagine them. The battles are always so fun to look at, and it feels epic to play.

But how do the battles feel in gameplay? Well the original is turn based, as many RPGs were in that day. The combat system is truly amazing in both games thanks to the materia system, which allows you to equip "Materia" with different spells and effects. I would say the turn based combat is pretty standard otherwise but its what makes those original Final Fantasy games so classic. The remake uses a real time action system where you can actually move around and perform whatever move you want whenever. Although turn based and real time are exact opposites, somehow I would say the original combat translates so well to the remake, because it really retains most everything else. What I will say is that I hated how much I was just spamming the normal attack button in the latter game, as turn based gives your actions much more weight. Both are equally fun, but I was less tired with the former.

Musically, the OG soundtrack is a classic, and the remake has amazing new renditions of those old tracks, you can tell how much effort and love was put in. Thanks to the extended length, the characters really shine in the remake, so well in fact I think it's worth playing just to see your favorite characters expanded on. The amount of love put into this remake is great...but there are some negatives.

The gameplay outside of the combat is not very good and has some of the worst trends in modern gaming like wretched fetch side quests and filler battles that feel like time wasters. The changes to the story can be a bit undercooked and can be confusing but I guess thats what the sequels are for. Granted the original has its own problems so all considered this isn't that bad. For a game that stretches a couple hours of the orignal into a whole game, it didn't fail, and I appreciate that they're taking their time on this, but the padding just kills it sometimes.

Overall, you can't really go wrong with either but I really would reccomend (for basically every game on this site) to play the original and then the remake, but for this entry, its specifically because the game assumes you already have experience with the game. I love both these games and I would say you should play FF7 any way you can (any version with toggle random encounters is great). But for the remake know playing the trilogy might take a couple hundreds hours of your life...Enjoy!
